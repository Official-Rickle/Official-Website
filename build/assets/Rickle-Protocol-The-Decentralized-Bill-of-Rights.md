Rickle Protocol: The Decentralized Bill of Rights

Article I: The Sovereignty of the Ledger
The ultimate truth of the protocol resides in the Live Chain Data. No marketing claim, founder statement, or community sentiment shall override the Live Trust Position. Transparency is the fundamental requirement.

Article II: The Right to Audit
Any participant has the perpetual right to Audit the Governance Smart Contracts. The source code, bytecode, and Unified Multisig balances must remain publicly accessible and verifiable via the RPC at all times.

Article III: Separation of Power
The protocol operates under a dual-layer system:

The Voting Layer: AHWA holders hold the power to propose and weight the direction of the treasury.

The Caretaker Layer: The 5 Multisig Signers act as the judicial branch, holding a veto to protect the treasury from impulsive or malicious attacks.

Article IV: Accountability & Election Sovereignty
Caretakers hold veto power over general treasury movements, but this power shall not extend to Elections. Any proposal to remove or replace a Caretaker/Signer shall be decided solely by the Staked AHWA Weight of the community. A veto cannot be used by the Caretakers to protect their own seat.

Article V: The Gold Standard Mandate
The treasury’s primary mission is the acquisition and maintenance of Gold Standard Assets (BTCB, ETH, etc.). The Backing Ratio target of 110% is the programmatic north star.

Article VI: Ownership of Capital
A hard line exists between Trust Treasury and Founder Capital:

The Unified Multisig (Trust): This is the community's decentralized bank. These assets are governed by AHWA and the Caretakers.

The Strategic Reserve (Founder): This is the Founder’s personal capital, used for Strategic Buy-Backs at the Founder's discretion. These funds and the tokens they acquire are the private property of the Founder. The Founder retains full rights to hold, retire, or sell these assets as they see fit. No promise of permanent retirement is made; the Founder's moves are personal market actions.

Article VII: The End of "Free"
Entry into the Decentralized Bank is a privilege earned through the acquisition of Rickle, WIN, or AHWA. Holders have the right to exit at market price, but the protocol’s duty is to ensure that price is backed by real-world, on-chain value.
