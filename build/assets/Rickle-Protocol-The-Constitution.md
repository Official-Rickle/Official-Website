Rickle Protocol: The Constitution

Article I: Supreme Rule of Law
This Constitution is the foundational legal framework for the Rickle Protocol.

Codified Logic: This document acts as the supreme rule of law, dictating how and what the protocol does.

Immutable Records: Any change to this Constitution, or any modification to the Decentralized Bill of Rights, must be ratified as an On-Chain Proposal. If a rule is not recorded on the blockchain, it is not recognized as law.

Article II: Amendments & The Absolute Threshold
To protect the protocol’s foundation from impulsive shifts or voter apathy:

The Absolute Threshold: Any proposal to amend or override this Constitution, or the Bill of Rights, is only valid if the "YES" votes represent a Super-Majority of the Total Circulating AHWA supply (defined as all AHWA not held by the Trust or Liquidity Pools).

The Veto Limit: The Caretaker Veto is a tool for treasury protection and operational integrity. The Veto cannot be used on elections. Any vote to add, remove, or replace a Caretaker is decided solely by the community; Caretakers are strictly prohibited from vetoing their own succession or removal.

Exclusion of Treasury: Assets held within the Unified Multisig are strictly non-voting. The bank cannot influence its own constitutional evolution.

Article III: The Caretaker Mandate (Operational Authority)
The Caretakers are the executive branch, tasked with protecting the trust and maintaining the ledger's truth.

Executive Power: Caretakers do not require a community vote for day-to-day operations, treasury management, or actions taken to defend the Trust’s integrity. Voting is for structural changes ("how/what") and new protocol directions.

Term Length: A single term is 5 Years, commencing only after the Caretaker Oath is taken.

Lifetime Term Limit: No individual—including the Founder—may serve more than 2 total terms (10 years) in their lifetime. Once the 10-year limit is reached, that individual is permanently ineligible for a Caretaker seat.

Quorum Integrity: The multisig must maintain an odd-number ratio: 3/5, 4/7, 6/11, or 7/13. There shall be no less than 5 Caretakers at any time.

Transition Protocol: Existing signers have 72 hours to update the multisig following a successful election.

Article IV: Execution Standards
Consensus Execution: All transactions require a majority of Caretaker Signatures based on the active quorum ratio.

Veto Specifics: Aside from elections, a veto requires near-unanimity (the threshold minus one) among Caretakers to override a community vote. Vetoes are identity-based and do not require the Caretaker to stake AHWA.

Article V: Ownership & The Strategic Reserve
The Trust Treasury: Managed for the protocol's benefit, aimed at the 110% Backing Ratio.

The Founder’s Reserve: The Strategic Reserve is the Founder's personal capital. The Founder retains total discretion over these funds and any RKL acquired through personal buy-backs. The Constitution holds no jurisdiction over the Founder's private property.
